Top Characters from Avatar: The Last Airbender

Iroh

"There is nothing wrong with letting people who love you help you."

Uncle Iroh is the funny, unassuming uncle, sent to babysit Prince Zuko in the middle of a frigid nowhere. In contrast to Zuko's feriocious and childish displays of fire-bending power, Iroah merely sits back and drinks tea. It's not until the conclusion of Book I that Iroh's true character shines through. We see a wise and loving, though imperfect, uncle. This is just the tip of the Iroh iceberg (er...teabag?).
Iroh is a hit from the start, but as the TV show progresses, we only love him more and more. We learn that he has a complicated past, that he loves Suko as his own, that he would sacrifice anything to save him. (Also, lightning?!) Besides being the perfect foil to Zuko's temper and providing comic relief at every stop, Iroh represents hope for humanity. He opposes the typical, power-hungry warlord, providing through word and deed that the best leaders rarely hold the most power; the best leaders stand for what is right. Iroh is proof that even from the heart of a jaded, old Fire Nation general, good shines within us all.

Appa

"Mwooooarrr."

Appa is who we all aspire to be like someday. The ever companion of Aang, this flying bison (buffalo?) has the (literally) biggest heart of any wind-bending creature out there. Appa is tireless in transporting Aang and his friends across the map, day and night. He always steps in to save the day, using his awesome might and strength. The possible last of his kind, he and Aang share a special, unbreakable bond. You want miss this reliable fuzzy pal in the sky.
Even though he can't speak, Appa communicates wonders. From his growls and yawns, to his blank, lovable expression, Appa is not short on feelings. No wonder we feel such pain when Appa is capture by the sand-benders in Book II! (Just kidding guys, we all knew he was gonna be fine.) Appa demonstrates the power of stoic determination and innocent love and loyalty.

Aang

"I laugh at gravity all the time. Hahaha, gravity."

Image from: Wikipedia

Overview
The Avatar, The Last Airbender, "His airbending skills are great."
Background
Aang grew up as an airbending monk. At a young age he learned he was the Avatar. Suddenly, one day, he vanished. One hundred years later, he was discovered by Katara and Sokka, South-pole Water Tribe kids, frozen in an iceberg along his flying bison, Appa. Aang is destined to save the world from the tyranny of the Fire Nation, but he has a lot of responsibity to own and a lot of growing up to do before he's ready.
Characteristics
Aang's most prominent characteristics are his playful nature, fealess determination, loyalty, courage, and pacifist style.
What Makes Them Great
In terms of fan-favorites, Aang isn't the best character in the series. Collective opinion has decided that beloved characters like Iroh and Toph take that cake. This is not an uncommon occurence, however. Rarely in movies or TV is the main character the favorite character, most likely because the protaganist can come off as robotic or bland. Not Aang. Even if he isn't #1 on the list, he deserves props for being a main character with real depth and personality. He hase vice and virute, strengths and weaknesses, opinions, and desires. He perfectly captures a happy, 12-year-old boy. He's a real human trying to cope with so heavy a burden while so many people depend on him. Somehow, the writers pull this off spectacularly. Again and again, we see Avatar taking it to the next level, and its titular character embodies that. Whether Aang is your favorite character or not, he's an exemplary protaganist with a lot of heart.

Katara

"It is the strength of your hearts that make you who you are."

Image from: Advanced Graphics

Overview
Waterbending master, believes Aang can "save the world".
Background
Born into the Southern Water Tribe, mother killed in the war, father in battle, lives with her knuckle-headed brother Sokka.
Characteristics
Natural leader, fiery temper, encourages others.
What Makes Them Great
Katara keeps Team Avatar together by acting as a diplomat and a mediator. But that doesn't meen she's a pushover. Katara fights for her beliefs and demands what she wants. She's not every to stand up to bullies, and she often is.
